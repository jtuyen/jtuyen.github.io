During these unprecedented times, we all know someone who lost their job because of COVID-19. It doesn't matter if you worked in hospitality or even tech, everyone got affected no matter which industry. I noticed in my Discord channels, there was an uptick of users seeking for new job opportunities and need help with breaking into cybersecurity. The problem is cybersecurity is a field where it's difficult to get into unless you have experience and/or have accredited degree(s)/certificate(s) that is highly sought for. Now, I'm not saying these are the only two ways to get into cybersecurity. The beauty of this field is you can beat your own drum such as becoming proficient in bug bounties, hosting capture the flag events, building opensource tools, even cybersecurity vlogging/streamer. Heck, you don't even need to be a technical genius to be selling cybersecurity products if that is your thing.

The reoccurring pattern I'm seeing for job seekers is the lack of using OSINT to your advantage and not able to stand out from the masses. Here are some tips that I thought was effective during my job hunt.

Don't try and focus all your effort using the common job search portals such as LinkedIn, Monster, Indeed, and etc. Mostly those job postings are for more mid-senior roles. You won't be able to stand out from the crowd if you don't have adequate working experience, certificates, and connections. What most people don't utilize often enough is using Twitter. It's the best way to find job opportunities that aren't posted on common job portals and at times posted by individuals who are already working for the company thus immediately bypassing the resume keyword filtering robots.

## Local InfoSec Scene
To get started, you need to find other information security professionals already in the field by going to your local BSides, OWASP, and popular conferences. Since COVID-19 happened, in-person meetups won't be possible anytime soon. This is where Twitter will save the day. If you visit your local BSides Twitter account, check the followers list, scroll to the beginning of the list.

![Twitter](/assets/images/personal/jobhuntosint/jobhuntosint-twitter.png){:height="600px"}

These select few individuals are most likely the founders or have close connections to the event coordinators. I would highly suggest contacting them with smart questions prepared or be genuinely curious. The InfoSec scene in general is receptive to help anyone who is asking so don't be shy and rob yourself of the opportunity to let yourself shine. Lastly, if you take a look at the rest of the Twitter follower list, you'll see other local InfoSec companies which you should take a look at for possible opportunities.

Two other quick tips about Twitter is using [TweetBeaver](https://tweetbeaver.com) to search through user timelines for possible job opportunities. It's a tedious task but it's a good method to determine who is a great user to be following for future jobs by setting alert notifications. If you are lazy like me, use the [Advanced Search](https://twitter.com/search-advanced?lang=en) feature for Twitter and narrow down the location and keywords you are looking for such as specific job titles and hash tags. The combination of these two powerful features should help get in touch with someone who you are looking for. 

Lastly, search through conference websites for sponsor pages. This is a great way to figure out other local InfoSec companies for possible job boards.
